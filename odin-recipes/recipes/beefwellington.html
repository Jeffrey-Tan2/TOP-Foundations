Beef Wellington

Description

Gordon Ramsay's version of the classic steak dish - a showstopping centrepiece on a special occasion

Ingredients

  • Beef fillet
  • Olive oil
  • Chestnut mushroom
  • Butter
  • Fresh thyme
  • Dry white wine
  • Prosciutto
  • Puff pastry
  • Flour
  • Egg yolk

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 220C
  2. Sit beef fillet on a roasting tray. Brush with olive oil and season with pepper
  3. Roast for 15 mins for medium-rare, 20 mins for medium
  4. While beef is cooling, chop 250g chestnut mushrooms as finely as possible
  5. Heat 2 tbsp of olive oil and 50g of butter in large pan and fry the mushroom on medium heat with thyme for 10 mins, stirring often
  6. Season mushroom mixture, pour over 100ml dry white wine and cook for 10 mins until wine has absorbed
    7. >
  7. Remove the mushroom duxelle from the pan to cool and discard the thyme
  8. Overlap two pieces of cling film over a large chopping board. Lay 12 slices prosciutto on the cling film, slightly overlapping, in a double row
  9. Spread half the duxelles over the prosciutto, then sit the fillet on it and spread the remaining duxelles over
  10. Use the cling film’s edges to draw the prosciutto around the fillet, then roll it into a sausage shape, twisting the ends of cling film to tighten it as you go Chill the fillet while you roll out the pastry Dust your work surface with a little flour. Roll out a third of the 500g pack of puff pastry to a 18 x 30cm strip and place on a non-stick baking sheet
  11. Roll out the remainder of the 500g pack of puff pastry to about 28 x 36cm Unravel the fillet from the cling film and sit it in the centre of the smaller strip of pastry Beat the 2 egg yolks with 1 tsp water and brush the pastry’s edges, and the top and sides of the wrapped fillet
  12. Using a rolling pin, carefully lift and drape the larger piece of pastry over the fillet, pressing well into the sides Trim the joins to about a 4cm rim. Seal the rim with the edge of a fork or spoon handle Glaze all over with more egg yolk and, using the back of a knife, mark the beef Wellington with long diagonal lines taking care not to cut into the pastry
  13. Chill for at least 30 mins and up to 24 hrs. Heat oven to 200C/fan 180C/gas 6 Brush the Wellington with a little more egg yolk and cook until golden and crisp – 20-25 mins for medium-rare beef, 30 mins for medium. Allow to stand for 10 mins before serving in thick slices
